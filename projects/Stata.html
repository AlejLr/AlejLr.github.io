Dutch Housing Hedonic Pricing (1995–2000)

Econometrics study quantifying how structure, location, and timing shape house prices in the Netherlands. Built a progressive OLS framework to surface the most decision-relevant drivers while balancing accuracy and interpretability.

What this shows

  • Ability to clean large real-estate datasets and engineer meaningful features for valuation.
  • Sound econometric modeling with robust SEs and fixed effects for time and region.
  • Clear model progression (baseline → quality → location → temporal) to communicate trade-offs.
  • Well-structured research deliverables: reproducible code, figures, and report.

Executive highlights

  • Space & quality premiums: volume, plot size, rooms, and maintenance drive value.
  • Location matters: urbanization and regional effects explain substantial variation.
  • Market dynamics: longer time-on-market correlates with lower realized prices.
  • Balanced choice: Model 4 used for interpretation (strong fit, transparent structure).

How it was built (at a glance)

  • Data preparation: plausibility filters, percentile trimming, log transforms for skewed variables.
  • Modeling: OLS with robust SEs; fixed effects for year and region; incremental specifications.
  • Artifacts: figures, tables, and final report for rapid stakeholder review.

Limitations & next steps

  • Neighborhood amenities and micro-spatial variation not directly observed.
  • Next: panel/spatial methods, environmental attributes, and richer accessibility data.
